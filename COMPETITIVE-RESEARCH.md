# Competitive research: the Colombia site + social landscape

Researched June 17, 2026 (World Cup debut day). How Colombian Central stacks up against other Colombia-themed sites and socials, and where the openings are. Three passes: a deep dive on colombiaone.com, the wider site landscape, and what works on Colombian diaspora social.

Follower numbers are approximate. Socials resist scraping, so treat them as directional, not exact.

## Headline

No one runs the all-in-one diaspora model. Every Colombia site is either editorial with no commerce (ColombiaOne, Colombia Reports, City Paper Bogotá) or commerce with no audience (Amigo Foods, coffee specialists, Etsy Wayuu sellers). Colombian Central's news + tienda + travel + affiliate + World Cup under one roof has no direct competitor. The wedge is the US "Colombian-American" identity (not "expat living in Colombia"), and the 2026 World Cup is the acquisition window.

## ColombiaOne.com (the closest competitor)

- **What it is:** English news + culture hub openly courting the US diaspora. Claims "No. 1 English publisher on Colombia," ~50% US traffic, NYC their top city, audience 18 to 34. Operated by 1ONE MEDIA LLC.
- **Structure:** daily, staffed newsroom with real bylines. Sections mirror ours: News, World Cup, Politics, Culture, Travel, Business, History, Food, Music, Sports. Solid SEO-clean journalism, not deeply original.
- **Tech/design:** WordPress + the stock tagDiv "Newspaper" theme. Generic, no distinctive brand.
- **Monetization (single-threaded):** Google AdSense (no premium ad manager, so modest RPM) + a productized **media kit** selling sponsorship tiers (banners, advertorials, sponsored series, branded video, newsletter slots) + newsletter. No shop, no travel, no affiliate, no membership.
- **Social (approx):** Instagram @colombiaonecom ~176K is the entire engine (daily news-graphic cards/carousels + Reels). TikTok ~32K, Facebook ~19K, X @co1ombia ~190 (dead, typo handle), YouTube small. Lopsided to IG.
- **Strengths:** clear English-Colombia niche, first-mover SEO, daily output, one strong IG channel, a real media kit.
- **Weaknesses:** thin revenue stack, one-platform risk, neglected/typo'd X, template design, English-only with token Spanish.

## The wider landscape

- **Colombia Reports** (English): the reference English news source since 2008, ~150K monthly uniques. Hard news/politics, not lifestyle. Reader-funded via Patreon, refuses sponsored content. No commerce.
- **The City Paper Bogotá** (English): largest/longest-running English paper, print + web. Arts, culture, expat living, dining. Bogotá expats/tourists. Ad-supported.
- **The Bogotá Post** (English): Bogotá-centric news for the international community since 2014. Ad-supported. Small social (~11K FB, medium confidence).
- **Pulzo** (Spanish): top-5 Colombian digital outlet, viral/general news, mass domestic audience. ~2.9M FB (medium-high confidence). Programmatic ads.
- **Las2Orillas** (Spanish): independent opinion/politics with user-generated notes. ~1M+ FB (medium confidence). Domestic, not diaspora.
- **Blu Radio** (Spanish): Caracol broadcaster, news/talk, domestic mass market.
- **Commerce field (fragmented):** Amigo Foods, Paisa USA, Venco Arepas, coffee specialists (Devoción, Juan Valdez US), Wayuu sellers on Etsy. All single-vertical product, no editorial, no community, no travel.

## Gap analysis

- **No all-in-one diaspora hub exists.** Editorial players have content but zero commerce or travel. Commerce players have product but no audience engine. Our content → tienda → travel → affiliate journey has no direct rival.
- **ColombiaOne is the one to watch:** the only outlet pairing English + explicit US-diaspora framing + full social/newsletter. Closest on content and audience, but it monetizes thinly and sells nothing. That gap is our wedge.
- **"Colombian-American" is underserved.** Existing English outlets serve foreigners living in Colombia (visas, Bogotá dining), not US-born/raised Colombians (Miami, Queens/Jackson Heights, the NJ "Colombian Corridor," second-gen identity, remittances home). ~1.8M US Colombians, the largest South American group in the US, lack a dedicated commercial hub.
- **World Cup 2026 is open white space.** Nobody is built around La Tricolor for a US fan audience. Time-boxed acquisition wedge.
- **Transactional intent is unmonetized.** Remittances, tours, fan gear are high-intent diaspora behaviors pure publishers leave on the table. We already target these with the money-pages.

## What to steal

1. One hero social channel done relentlessly (their IG). News-as-graphic-cards/carousels + Reels, daily.
2. A productized media kit: sponsorship tiers, once we have an audience number to pitch.
3. First-mover English SEO on Colombia topics (World Cup, players, elections, evergreen guides).

## What to do better

1. **Commerce-as-content is the moat:** read about a region → buy its coffee/craft → book the trip. Cross-link hard.
2. **Keep the design edge:** the kiosco editorial system (Anton, tricolor, hard ink shadows) beats their template. Cheap leverage.
3. **Own bilingual warmth:** Spanish for warmth, English for information, visible in captions and newsletter, not just site copy.
4. **Newsletter that sells:** culture + tienda drops + travel, the owned retention channel they don't use commercially.
5. **Don't spread thin:** win Instagram first; avoid their dead-platform mistake.

## Social playbook (US Colombian diaspora)

- Engagement runs on **food nostalgia + fútbol**, in warm Spanglish. Loyalty is to cultural resonance, not brands.
- Formats that travel: silent recipe reels (arepa/ajiaco/sancocho), "you know you're Colombian if…" lists, nostalgia montages (Pony Malta, Colombiana, Encanto), regional-pride/rivalry bits (paisa vs costeño vs rolo), live fútbol reactions + memes, abuela-secret-recipe reveals. Reels drive 50%+ of IG engagement; the algorithm rewards saves + shares + watch time.
- Reference accounts: My Colombian Recipes (@mycolombianrecipes), Devoción (@devocionusa), fan accounts like @latricolor_fc.
- Commerce angles that convert: Devoción's farm-to-cup freshness story, Juan Valdez heritage, Wayuu "14 days to weave one bag" artisan-direct framing, the 2026 kit's García Márquez yellow-butterfly tribute.
- Cadence: TikTok + IG Reels first, 1x/day minimum, 2-3x on match days with live reactions.
